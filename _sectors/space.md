## Why Now

- **Launch cost collapse**: SpaceX reusability has dropped cost-to-orbit by ~95% since 2010; Rocket Lab and new entrants are commoditizing small-sat access
- **Satellite internet** (Starlink, OneWeb, Amazon Kuiper) is scaling to hundreds of millions of subscribers — ground infrastructure and spectrum plays benefit
- **DoD and NASA budget** prioritization shifting to commercial providers under COTS/CLPS frameworks; government as anchor customer de-risks early revenue
- **Lunar economy** is nascent but NASA's Artemis program and CLPS awards are real, near-term contracts worth hundreds of millions

## Key Risks

- **SpaceX private** — the dominant player is not investable; public comps are second-order beneficiaries
- **Regulatory and spectrum fights** could slow satellite broadband deployments (FCC, ITU)
- **Execution risk** is extreme for pre-revenue or early-revenue space companies; timelines routinely slip 12–24 months
- **Capital intensity** — space hardware is expensive; dilution risk is high for smaller players

## Catalysts to Watch

- Rocket Lab Neutron launch vehicle development milestones
- NASA CLPS task order awards (lunar lander missions)
- AST SpaceMobile commercial launch count and subscriber activations
- Starlink IPO rumors (private — any news re-rates public comps)
