## Why Now

- **AI training and inference** demand is structurally expanding GPU, HBM, and advanced packaging supply chains — not a cyclical spike
- **Sovereign AI buildouts** (EU, Japan, Middle East) are adding a second demand wave independent of US hyperscalers
- **Leading-edge concentration**: only TSMC, Samsung, and Intel Foundry can manufacture at 3nm or below — scarcity premium is durable
- **Power bottleneck** is shifting spend toward energy-efficient silicon (custom ASICs, edge inference chips)

## Key Risks

- **Export controls** on advanced chips to China can compress addressable market by ~15–20% overnight
- **TSMC geopolitical risk** — ~90% of leading-edge wafers are in Taiwan; any escalation re-prices the sector
- **Inventory digestion** in PC/mobile/auto could weigh on mid-tier fabs (TSMC N5/N7 utilization)

## Catalysts to Watch

- TSMC monthly revenue reports (leading indicator for AI demand)
- NVIDIA GTC announcements and data center guidance
- US CHIPS Act fab construction milestones (Intel Ohio, TSMC Arizona)
- China export control expansions or rollbacks
