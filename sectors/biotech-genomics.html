Why Now

  • AI is collapsing drug discovery timelines: AlphaFold solved the protein folding problem; AI models can now screen billions of compounds in silico vs years of wet lab work
  • CRISPR clinical validation: the FDA approval of Casgevy (CRISPR/Vertex) in 2023 proved gene editing works in humans — the pipeline is now expanding beyond blood disorders
  • GLP-1 wave (Ozempic, Mounjaro) has proven the biotech sector can generate trillion-dollar drug categories — investor appetite for next-generation biologics is elevated
  • mRNA platform versatility: COVID vaccines proved mRNA can be designed and manufactured in months; the same platform is now targeting cancer, flu, HIV, and rare diseases

Key Risks

  • Clinical trial binary risk: biotech is inherently high-variance; a single Phase 3 failure can wipe 50–80% of a company's value overnight
  • Drug pricing regulation: IRA drug price negotiation provisions could compress margins on approved drugs
  • AI drug discovery is unproven at scale: most AI-designed drugs have not yet completed Phase 3 trials; the promise is real but the evidence base is thin
  • Execution concentration: many plays depend on 1–2 pipeline assets; diversification within the sector is important

Catalysts to Watch

  • CRISPR Therapeutics and Vertex Casgevy launch uptake data (commercial validation of gene editing)
  • Recursion Pharma Phase 2 trial readouts (first major test of AI drug discovery)
  • Illumina long-read sequencing competitive response to Oxford Nanopore and PacBio
  • FDA approvals in the mRNA pipeline (Moderna RSV, flu, cancer vaccines)
  • Major pharma AI partnership announcements (Pfizer, Roche, AstraZeneca licensing AI platforms)
