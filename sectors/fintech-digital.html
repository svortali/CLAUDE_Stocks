Why Now

  • Digital banking penetration in Latin America, Southeast Asia, and Africa is still sub-30% — the multi-decade growth runway for neobanks like Nubank dwarfs what's already been captured
  • Crypto regulatory clarity is finally emerging in the US: Bitcoin spot ETFs approved, stablecoin legislation moving — institutionalization removes the existential risk that has weighed on the sector
  • AI in financial services: fraud detection, underwriting, and personalized financial advice are being rebuilt on AI — incumbents and challengers both benefit
  • Stablecoin adoption for cross-border payments is growing rapidly in emerging markets — companies with stablecoin rails (Coinbase, Circle) are early beneficiaries

Key Risks

  • Crypto cyclicality: COIN and SQ are heavily correlated to Bitcoin price; a bear market compresses revenue significantly
  • Regulatory risk remains: despite progress, crypto regulation is still evolving globally; a hostile regime in a key market can hurt quickly
  • Disintermediation risk: central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) could displace some fintech use cases, especially in emerging markets
  • Credit cycle exposure: neobanks like Nubank face higher credit losses in a recession given their customer base is often sub-prime or underbanked

Catalysts to Watch

  • US stablecoin legislation (GENIUS Act or equivalent) — creates a legal framework for USD stablecoins
  • Bitcoin price cycle (halving was April 2024; historically 12–18 months to peak)
  • Nubank Brazil credit quality metrics in earnings (watch NPL ratio)
  • Coinbase institutional custody growth (signals Wall Street crypto adoption)
  • PayPal stablecoin (PYUSD) adoption and Venmo monetization announcements
