Why Now

  • AI power crisis: a single large GPU cluster consumes as much power as a small city; hyperscalers are signing 20-year nuclear PPAs to secure firm, carbon-free power
  • Nuclear is the only always-on, zero-carbon power source at scale — wind and solar can't meet the 24/7 baseload needs of data centers
  • SMR revolution: small modular reactors (Oklo, NuScale, TerraPower) promise factory-built, lower-cost nuclear within this decade; regulatory path is opening
  • Uranium supply constraints: global uranium production has underinvested for 15 years; new demand from existing + new reactors will stress supply chains
  • Grid instability in the US and Europe is creating premium pricing for firm power — nuclear operators are repricing contracts at 2–3x legacy rates

Key Risks

  • Regulatory timeline risk for SMRs — NRC licensing is slow and SMR cost projections have historically been optimistic
  • Construction cost overruns have plagued large nuclear builds (Vogtle in the US came in billions over budget)
  • Political risk — anti-nuclear sentiment in some states/countries could constrain permitting even with supportive federal policy
  • Renewables + storage cost curve: if grid-scale batteries improve faster than expected, the nuclear advantage for baseload diminishes

Catalysts to Watch

  • Hyperscaler nuclear PPA announcements (follow Amazon, Google, Microsoft press releases)
  • Oklo and NuScale NRC licensing milestones
  • US Department of Energy loan guarantee awards for nuclear projects
  • Uranium spot price (Cameco and uranium ETFs like URA are real-time indicators)
  • Three Mile Island Unit 1 restart timeline (Constellation / Microsoft deal)
