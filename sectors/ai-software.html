Why Now

  • Infrastructure layer is winning now: hyperscalers (MSFT Azure, AWS, GCP) capturing AI spend regardless of which model wins
  • Advertising duopoly (Meta, Google) using AI to increase ad yield — measurable, near-term revenue impact
  • Enterprise AI adoption is early but accelerating; Copilot and similar tools reaching critical mass in large organizations
  • Caution: pure-play AI software valuations (Palantir, C3.ai, etc.) price in 10+ years of perfect execution — margin of safety is thin

Key Risks

  • Model commoditization could compress margins for API-dependent businesses if frontier model costs fall rapidly
  • Regulatory risk (EU AI Act, US antitrust scrutiny of hyperscalers) introduces headline risk
  • Open-source disruption — Llama and similar models allow enterprises to run AI without paying API fees
  • Valuation compression if AI monetization timelines extend beyond 2026–2027

Catalysts to Watch

  • Microsoft Copilot seat count and ARPU disclosures in earnings
  • Meta AI MAU announcements (Llama adoption, standalone AI app)
  • OpenAI IPO timeline (private — any progress re-rates the sector)
  • Enterprise AI contract wins at Palantir, Salesforce, ServiceNow
